Space trains.

Those two words compelled me to pick up a secondhand copy of Night Train to Rigel by Timothy Zahn. At the time I’d just moved to Sydney with my wife — we were house hunting, I was searching for a co-working space and adjusting to a new life in a new city. Reviews | #1 in Moties

The Mote In God's Eye book review

Published in 1974, The Mote In God’s Eye was a runaway success. It fused the space opera of Star Trek, the world building of Dune, and the militaristic elements of Starship Troopers. It’s 537 pages of space faring goodness. And if you think Europe has a lot of museums, just wait until you get to Mote Prime. Unlike Star Trek, Dune and Starship Troopers, The Mote In God’s Eye shows its age.
